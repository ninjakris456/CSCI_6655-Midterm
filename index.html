ABOUT ME

Albert Einstein was a German-born physicist who is widely regarded as one of the most influential scientists of the 20th century. He is best known for his theory of relativity, which revolutionized our understanding of space, time, and gravity.
Einstein was born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Germany, and grew up in Munich. He studied physics and mathematics at the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology (ETH) in Zurich and later worked as a patent clerk in Bern, Switzerland.
In 1905, Einstein published four groundbreaking papers that laid the foundation for modern physics. These papers introduced the theory of special relativity, which explains how time and space are relative to the observer's perspective, and the famous equation E=mc², which shows the equivalence of mass and energy.
Later, Einstein developed the theory of general relativity, which explains the force of gravity as a curvature in spacetime caused by the presence of massive objects. His work also contributed to the development of quantum mechanics, which explains the behavior of particles on the atomic and subatomic scale.
In addition to his contributions to physics, Einstein was a pacifist and a social activist, speaking out against nuclear weapons and working towards world peace. He emigrated to the United States in 1933 to escape Nazi persecution and became a US citizen in 1940. Einstein died on April 18, 1955, at the age of 76.

Albert Einstein remains a towering figure in the world of science, and his theories continue to inspire new research and breakthroughs to this day.

EDUCATION

University of Bern - PHD

Graduated with a PHD. Wrote dissertation on the determination of molecular dimensions.

University of Zurich - PHD

Graduated with a Degree in Mathematics and Natural Sciences. From 1909, he was an Extraordinary Professor at the University of Zurich and from 1912 to 1914 he worked as a Professor of Theoretical Physics at ETH Zürich.

Swiss Federal Polytechnic - Bachelor's degree

1896 - 1900, attended ETH Zurich for a BA in physics.

Luitpold Gymnasium - Schooling

Studied various subjects at Luitpold Gymnasium, which was a secondary school in Munich, Germany. The subjects he studied included mathematics, physics, Latin, Greek, and history.

HOBBIES

Playing Violin

Einstein was an accomplished violinist and began playing at a young age. He continued to play throughout his life and was known to enjoy performing classical music in his spare time.

Reading

Einstein was an avid reader and had a personal library of over 2,000 books. He was interested in a wide range of subjects, from science and philosophy to literature and history.

Sailing

Einstein loved to sail and owned a boat named Tümmler. He would often spend weekends on the water, enjoying the peacefulness and beauty of nature.

Traveling

Einstein was an avid traveler and visited many countries throughout his lifetime. He was particularly fascinated by Asia and the Middle East, and often incorporated his travels into his work as a physicist.
